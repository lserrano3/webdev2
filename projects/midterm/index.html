Overview

The 17th-century Amsterdam canals are a world renowned location of cultural and historical significance with a rich history spanning 400 years of development, expansion, innovation and engineering. The city's canal ring and its design is an early example of large-scale, coordinated urban planning and forward thinking.

NOORD
HOLLAND

The city of Amsterdam is located in Noord-Holland, the northwestern province of the Netherlands. It's the capital city and has a population of around 850,000 in the main city area and more than 2 million in the wider metropolitan area, being the largest and most lived-in city in the country. With its famous canal belt and very well preserved 17th century architecture, the city thrives on tourism bringing in many millions of visitors each year. The summer months from June to August see the biggest influx of visitors who can experience the best of the canals by boat, through a time of late sunsets and lively city atmosphere. As well as its global visitors, it’s one of the world's most multicultural cities with over 170 nationalities.
